What Is a Good Loan APR? Rates by Loan Type (2026)

APR (Annual Percentage Rate) is the true cost of borrowing — it includes both the interest rate and any fees. Knowing what's a "good" APR for each loan type can save you thousands of dollars over the life of a loan.

APR vs Interest Rate: What's the Difference?

The interest rate is the base cost of borrowing. APR adds lender fees, points, and other charges — giving you the real annual cost. Always compare APRs, not interest rates, when shopping for loans.

The Real Cost of APR Differences

$300,000 mortgage, 30 years: At 6.0% APR, total cost = $647,000. At 7.0% APR, total cost = $718,000. That 1% difference costs $71,000 extra over the loan's life.

How to Get the Best APR

  1. Improve your credit score — above 740 gets the best rates
  2. Shop multiple lenders — compare at least 3 offers
  3. Consider points — paying upfront points can lower your APR on mortgages
  4. Shorter terms = lower APRs — 15-year mortgages have significantly lower rates than 30-year
  5. Use a loan comparison calculator — see the total cost difference between offers

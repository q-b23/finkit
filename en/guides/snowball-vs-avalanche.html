Snowball vs Avalanche: Which Debt Payoff Strategy Is Better?

June 2026 · FinKit · 5 min read

If you're juggling multiple debts — credit cards, personal loans, car loans — you've probably heard of two major repayment strategies: 雪球法(Debt Snowball)和雪崩法(Debt Avalanche). But which one is right for you? This guide will help you decide.

什么是雪球法(Debt Snowball)？

The core logic: sort debts from smallest to largest balance. Pay off the smallest debt first while making minimum payments on others. Each cleared debt is a psychological "win" that builds momentum.

How to Use the Snowball Method

  1. List all debts, sorted by balance (smallest first)
  2. Make minimum payments on every debt
  3. Apply your extra monthly budget entirely toward the smallest debt
  4. After the first debt is cleared, roll its payment (minimum + extra) into the next smallest debt
  5. Repeat until all debts are gone

Snowball Advantages

  • Strong psychological motivation — quick wins build confidence and habits
  • Reduces complexity — one fewer account to manage with each payoff
  • Easier to stick with — research shows people persist longer when they see frequent wins

Snowball Disadvantages

  • If small debts have low rates but large debts have high rates, you may pay more in total interest
  • Not mathematically optimal

什么是雪崩法(Debt Avalanche)？

The core logic: sort debts from highest to lowest APR. Target the highest-interest debt first. This is the mathematically optimal strategy for minimizing interest.

How to Use the Avalanche Method

  1. List all debts, sorted by APR (highest first)
  2. Make minimum payments on every debt
  3. Apply your extra monthly budget entirely toward the highest-APR debt
  4. After clearing the first, roll its payment into the next highest-APR debt
  5. Repeat until all debts are gone

Avalanche Advantages

  • Lowest total interest — the mathematical optimum
  • Potentially faster total payoff — less interest means more principal repaid

Avalanche Disadvantages

  • If the highest-rate debt is also the largest, it may take a long time to see a debt cleared
  • Requires more discipline — early progress can look slow

💡 Harvard Business Review Research Finding

Behavioral economics research found that snowball users are significantly more likely to stick with the plan and pay off all debt，即使他们在数学上付出更多利息.原因很简单：execution beats optimization..

Real-World Example

Suppose you have three debts:

  • 信用卡 A：$15,000，利率 18%，月Lowest $450
  • 信用卡 B：$30,000，利率 25%，月Lowest $900
  • 消费贷：$80,000，利率 12%，月Lowest $2,000

Monthly extra budget: $2,000.

雪球法优先攻击信用卡 A（余额最小），雪崩法优先攻击信用卡 B（利率最高）.由于信用卡 B 恰好利率最高且余额第二小，两种策略的还款顺序不同：雪球法先还 A 再 B 再消费贷，雪崩法先还 B 再 A 再消费贷.

How to Choose?

  1. Calculate the interest difference first — if the difference is small (under 5%), pick the one you'll stick with
  2. Be honest about your personality — do you need quick wins, or are you a pure optimizer?
  3. You can combine them — clear 1-2 small debts with snowball for confidence, then switch to avalanche for high-rate debts

The most important thing: start now.无论选择哪种策略，持续执行都比策略本身更重要.
